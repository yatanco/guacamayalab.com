Essay: On the Frustration of Alzheimers Care and the Promise of Decentralized Science (DeSci)

When you, or your loved one, gets diagnosed with Alzheimer’s Disease the road is lonely and confusing.
Let me explain with my own experience.
A few years ago my sister and me started noticing that my mother was changing, not only was she forgetting certain things, but she was afraid of some things she never had been. I started getting worried, my sister saw it more as an age related normality.
So we decided to dig a bit deeper.

What do you do? You go to the neurologist, they say, lets run some tests.
Result: Some memory issues, lets call this mild cognitive impairment (MCI), Could be early stages of Alzheimers Disease.
Me: OK, so my mom has this, so what can we do about it doc?
Doc: Well nothing really, theres some medication that might slow it down or might not do anything and have sideeffects.
Me: Might? OK, and why does this happen?
Doc: We don’t really know, there’s some theories but no successful treatment
Me: So what do you recommend us to do?
Doc: Come back so we can monitor it…
Me: That’s it? You are not going to recommend anything?
Doc: Yes, come back so we can monitor it…

So we left the neurologists office, knowing that we don’t quite know what it is, there is no real treatment and no recommendations. Basically just “wait it out” and “be patient” till my mom no longer remembers her own name.
So no help on the medical side except some not very promising and not very effective medication.

I couldn’t just accept that, so I started looking for other ways to help my mother. And I found some things that helped my mothers life quality, such as the ketoflex diet and having a personal trainer and simplifying her space,  but nothing that would really change the outcome of this terrible disease,
